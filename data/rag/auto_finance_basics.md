# Auto Financing Basics for Passenger Cars

This document provides background knowledge for the AutoGenie RAG system. It is not meant to be exhaustive, but covers common concepts for new passenger car buyers.

## Key concepts

### On-road price vs ex-showroom price

- Ex-showroom price usually refers to the price of the car before taxes, registration, and insurance.
- On-road price includes ex-showroom price plus local taxes, registration, road tax, handling charges, and mandatory insurance.
- When estimating a loan, buyers should clarify whether the loan is taken on the on-road price or just the ex-showroom price.

### Down payment

- The down payment is the amount paid upfront by the buyer.
- A higher down payment reduces the principal amount of the loan and can significantly reduce total interest paid over the tenure.
- Many lenders require a minimum down payment percentage (for example, 10–25% of the on-road price).

### Loan tenure

- Common tenures for car loans range from 3 to 7 years.
- Shorter tenures lead to higher monthly payments but lower total interest.
- Longer tenures lower the monthly payment but increase the total interest cost.

### Interest rate

- Car loans can have fixed or floating interest rates.
- Fixed rate loans keep the same monthly payment throughout the tenure.
- Floating rate loans may change with market conditions, impacting future EMIs.

### EMI (Equated Monthly Installment)

- EMI is the fixed monthly payment that covers both principal and interest.
- The standard amortization formula is used to calculate EMI based on principal, rate, and tenure.

### Total cost of ownership

Beyond the purchase price and loan cost, buyers should consider:

- Fuel or charging costs
- Scheduled maintenance and service
- Insurance premiums
- Depreciation and resale value

## Rules of thumb

- Keeping tenure around 5 years often balances EMI affordability and total interest.
- Try to keep EMI under 25–30% of net monthly income for financial comfort.
- Electric vehicles may have higher upfront costs but lower running costs.
